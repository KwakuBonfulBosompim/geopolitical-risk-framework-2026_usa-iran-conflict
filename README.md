Geopolitical Risk Intelligence Framework: 2026 Conflict Scenario Analysis

📈 Executive Summary
This project presents a multi-layered financial stress-test modeling the impact of a simulated 2026 geopolitical conflict in the Middle East. By synthesizing cross-asset correlations, the framework analyzes how energy supply chokepoints (Strait of Hormuz) redistribute global wealth across **Commodities**, **Sovereign Credit**, and **Frontier Equity Markets** (specifically the Ghana Stock Exchange).

🚀 Key Insights
- Frontier Resilience: Identified the GSE Composite Index as a top performer (0.94%) during global downturns due to gold-export hedges.
- Supply-Chain Shock: Modeled a 15% global oil supply trap, driving Brent Crude to a projected $200/bbl.
- Sovereign Risk: Tracked credit spread volatility, identifying Ghana's -94 bps improvement vs. South Africa's +105 bps increase.

🛠 Tools & Methodology
- Analytics: Python (Pandas/Matplotlib) for market performance and volatility (VIX) correlation.
- Synthesis: NotebookLM for geopolitical blockade infographics and flowcharts.
- Data Sourcing: Yahoo Finance API for multi-year historical data on S&P 500, Gold, and Lithium.
